# Payroll and Compensation Requirements

## Overview
The Payroll and Compensation module of the HRMS (Human Resource Management System) is crucial for managing employee compensation, ensuring compliance with tax regulations, and providing employees with accurate payslips. This document outlines the functional requirements for this module.

## Functional Requirements

### 1. Salary Processing
- Ability to define different salary structures for various employee levels.
- Support for both fixed and variable salary components.
- Calculation of gross salary, deductions, and net pay on a monthly basis.
- Support for salary adjustments based on performance reviews, promotions, and market changes.

### 2. Tax Calculations
- Automatic calculation of applicable taxes based on government regulations.
- Ability to handle multiple tax brackets and deductions.
- Generation of tax reports for both employees and the organization.
- Compliance with local, state, and federal tax regulations.

### 3. Payslips
- Generation of payslips for each payroll cycle, detailing gross salary, deductions, and net pay.
- Ability for employees to view and download their payslips via the employee portal.
- Provision for historical payslip access for up to 5 years.

### 4. Statutory Compliance
- Ensure compliance with labor laws and regulations related to payroll.
- Automatic updates for compliance changes as laws change.
- Generation of mandatory reports for regulatory bodies, including tax authorities and labor departments.

### 5. Integration with Employee Records
- Seamless integration with the Employee Management module to pull employee data, such as leaves, attendance, and performance ratings.
- Ability to update employee payroll details based on changes in job status or personal information.
- Synchronization with bank systems for direct deposit of salaries.

### 6. User Roles and Permissions
- Different access levels for HR staff, finance teams, and employees.
- Ability to restrict access to sensitive payroll information based on user roles.

### 7. Audit and Reporting
- Maintain audit logs for all payroll transactions and changes.
- Generate periodic reports for financial analysis and auditing purposes.
